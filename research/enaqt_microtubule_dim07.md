## 1. Executive Summary

The hypothesis that microtubules support functionally relevant quantum biological effects spans a spectrum from well-established physical phenomena to highly speculative theoretical proposals. This analysis identifies **three distinct tiers of claims**:

**Tier 1 — Established Physical Phenomena (High Confidence)**:
- Tryptophan networks in microtubules exhibit collective optical behavior (superradiance/subradiance) observable via fluorescence spectroscopy. The Kurian et al. 2024 paper (J. Phys. Chem. B) provides experimental confirmation of enhanced quantum yield in tryptophan mega-networks. These are legitimate quantum optical effects in biological structures at thermal equilibrium.

**Tier 2 — Theoretically Interesting but Unverified (Medium Confidence)**:
- QED cavity models of microtubules (Mavromatos et al. 2025) propose decoherence times of O(10^-6) s based on ordered water-tubulin dipole interactions. These are sophisticated theoretical constructs with proposed experimental tests (Rabi splitting spectroscopy) but remain unverified.
- Fröhlich coherence in microtubules has been claimed experimentally by Bandyopadhyay's group but independent replication is lacking.
- Subradiant states could persist for tens of nanoseconds, which is physically plausible but functionally unexplained.

**Tier 3 — Highly Speculative / Critically Challenged (Low Confidence)**:
- The Hameroff-Penrose Orch OR model proposing quantum computation in microtubules as the basis for consciousness faces fundamental critiques from decoherence calculations (Tegmark 2000: 10^-13 to 10^-20 s; Naskar & Joarder 2023: ~10^-21 s).
- Claims of quantum teleportation in tryptophan chains (Shirmovsky) are theoretical modeling exercises without experimental validation.
- Scalable ambient-temperature quantum computation in microtubules remains purely theoretical.

## 2. The 2025 EPJ Plus QED Cavity Paper

### Mavromatos et al. (2025) — "On the Potential of Microtubules for Scalable Quantum Computation"

```
Claim: Microtubule interiors can be treated as high-Q quantum electrodynamics (QED) cavities that support decoherence-resistant entangled states under physiological conditions, with decoherence times of O(10^-6) s. Solitonic excitations mediate dissipation-free energy transfer along microtubule filaments, enabling scalable ambient-temperature quantum computation.
Source: European Physical Journal Plus
URL: https://epjplus.epj.org/articles/epjplus/abs/2025/11/13360_2025_Article_7022/13360_2025_Article_7022.html
Date: November 19, 2025
Excerpt: "In the physical model proposed by the authors, the microtubule interiors are treated as high-Q quantum electrodynamics (QED) cavities that can support decoherence-resistant entangled states under physiological conditions, with decoherence times of the order of O(10^-6) s. We identify strong electric dipole interactions between tubulin dimers and ordered water dipole quanta within the microtuble interior as the mechanism responsible for the extended coherence times."
Context: The paper integrates QED-based models with quantum information theory, proposing microtubules as multi-level qudit processors. The fundamental unit is a quDit encoded in tubulin dipole states. The paper proposes Rabi-splitting spectroscopy and entangled surface plasmon probes as experimental validations.
Confidence: LOW — The paper is entirely theoretical. The 10^-6 s decoherence time depends critically on assumptions about ordered water behavior inside microtubules and an effective permittivity parameter. No experimental confirmation is provided. The claim of "scalable ambient-temperature quantum computation" is extraordinary and requires extraordinary evidence.
```

**Key Mechanism**: The model proposes that ordered water in the MT lumen acts as a high-quality electromagnetic cavity, bounded by tubulin dipole quanta. Strong dipole-dipole interactions between tubulin dimers and ordered-water dipoles near the walls create an isolated cavity. Environmental coupling is primarily due to "leakage of water dipole quanta through the imperfect protein walls."

**Critical Assessment**:
1. The ordered water assumption is critical but controversial — while water ordering near hydrophilic surfaces is established, the extent of ordering in the MT lumen remains debated.
2. The decoherence time of 10^-6 s is ~15 orders of magnitude longer than standard calculations (Tegmark 2000) and requires dramatic suppression of environmental coupling.
3. The model proposes solitons propagating at ~155 m/s, traversing a micron-scale MT in ~10^-8 s, which "lies comfortably" within the decoherence window — but this is a theoretical prediction, not an observation.
4. The paper acknowledges that the model depends on "best available values for the salient physicochemical parameters" which may not accurately reflect the complex intracellular environment.

## 3. Celardo et al. 2019 — Superradiant Excitonic States

```
Claim: Tryptophan molecules arranged in their native microtubule configuration exhibit a superradiant lowest exciton state, which represents an excitation fully extended on the chromophore lattice. Long-lived subradiant states can be concentrated on the inner wall of the microtubule lumen, potentially maintaining excitonic transfer processes.
Source: New Journal of Physics
URL: https://doi.org/10.1088/1367-2630/aaf839
Date: February 2019
Excerpt: "Our analysis has shown that the coupling between tryptophan molecules is able to create a superradiant ground state, similar to the physical behavior of several photosynthetic antenna systems. Such a superradiant ground state, which absorbs in the UV spectral range, has been shown to be a coherent excitonic state extended over the whole microtubule lattice of tryptophan molecules. Interestingly, the superradiant ground state appears to be delocalized on the exterior wall of the microtubule, which interfaces with the cytoplasm... At the same time, we have shown that long-lived (hundreds of milliseconds) subradiant states can be concentrated on the inner wall of the microtubule lumen, potentially maintaining excitonic transfer processes across the cytoskeletal network."
Context: This paper used a Hamiltonian widely employed in quantum optics to describe light-matter interactions, with tryptophan positions and dipole orientations from realistic structural models of tubulin.
Confidence: MEDIUM — The theoretical framework is sound (standard quantum optics Hamiltonian applied to realistic structures). The existence of superradiant and subradiant states in ordered chromophore networks is well-established physics. However, the functional significance for biological information processing remains speculative. The "hundreds of milliseconds" subradiant lifetime claim is for the most subradiant state and may not be physiologically relevant.
```

**Critical Assessment**:
- The paper correctly applies established quantum optical theory to a biological structure.
- Superradiant states are predicted to be delocalized on the MT exterior (cytoplasmic interface), while subradiant states concentrate on the inner wall.
- The "supertransfer" coupling between lowest exciton states of smaller blocks enhances photoexcitation spreading velocity.
- Key limitation: The analysis is in the single-excitation manifold. Multi-excitation effects, exciton-phonon coupling, and explicit non-radiative decay channels are not fully treated.

## 4. PMC 2025 — Quantum Information Flow in Microtubule Tryptophan Networks

```
Claim: Using a Lindblad master equation approach, superradiant components drive rapid export of correlations to the environment, while subradiant components retain them. Scaling to larger ordered lattices strengthens both export and retention channels, whereas static disorder suppresses long-range transport. Information backflow (non-Markovianity) is observed, showing that correlations are not irreversibly lost.
Source: PMC (Frontiers in Physics)
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12938935/
Date: 2025
Excerpt: "Superradiant components drive the rapid export of correlations to the environment, whereas subradiant components retain them and slow their leakage. Embedding single tubulin units into larger dimers and spirals reshapes pairwise correlation maps and enables site-selective routing. Scaling to larger ordered lattices strengthens both export and retention channels, whereas static energetic and structural disorder suppresses long-range transport and reduces overall correlation transfer."
Context: This is the most sophisticated open quantum systems treatment of microtubule tryptophan networks to date, going beyond effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonians to use a proper Lindblad master equation.
Confidence: MEDIUM — The Lindbladian approach is more physically consistent than prior non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian treatments. However, the model still assigns a common transition energy and bare radiative rate to all tryptophans, and uses a single phenomenological nonradiative rate. The functional significance of the observed information backflow for neuronal signaling remains speculative.
```

**Key Findings**:
1. **Superradiant eigenstate**: Populations decay synchronously within ~1 ns. L1 coherence drops sharply. Transient entanglement appears briefly.
2. **Subradiant eigenstate**: Populations remain for tens of nanoseconds. Entanglement remains stable with gradual attenuation and oscillatory structure.
3. **Information backflow**: Non-Markovian backflow measure N(k) varies with local geometry, showing structured reservoir effects.
4. **Disorder effects**: Static energetic and structural disorder suppresses long-range transport.

## 5. Shirmovsky's Quantum Teleportation in Microtubules

### 5a. Shirmovsky & Chizhov 2023 — Entangled State Transfer

```
Claim: The propagation speed of excited states and associated entanglement transfer along microtubule tryptophan chains falls within the range of axonal conduction velocities (suggesting quantum-assisted signaling). Microtubules can be considered as signaling systems transmitting information via quantum channels.
Source: Biosystems
URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37400052/
Date: September 2023
Excerpt: "The paper shows that the excited states propagation rate falls within the range of nerve impulse velocity. It was shown that such a process also causes a transfer of quantum entanglement between tryptophans, so that microtubules can be considered as signaling system, the basis for transmitting information via the quantum channel."
Context: Models single-excitation migration along tryptophan chains with dipole-dipole interaction.
Confidence: LOW — This is a theoretical simulation only. The claim that propagation speed matches nerve impulse velocity is a numerical coincidence, not evidence of quantum-assisted signaling in vivo. No experimental validation exists.
```

### 5b. Shirmovsky 2024 — Quantum Teleportation Protocol

```
Claim: A quantum teleportation protocol can be defined in microtubule tryptophan chains, transferring an unknown quantum state from one tryptophan to another without physically transferring the state itself. The process can be defined as an analogue of the superdense coding protocol.
Source: Biosystems
URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38072337/
Date: January 2024
Excerpt: "The work implements the idea of defining a quantum teleportation process in a biosystem — in the cell microtubules. The protocol defined in this paper determines the mechanism for transferring an unknown quantum state from one tryptophan to another without physically transferring the state itself, as well as without of human involvement."
Context: Uses quantum gates representation (Pauli X, Z, Hadamard, CNOT) to model tryptophan excitations as qubits.
Confidence: LOW — This is a formal mapping of quantum information protocols onto a hypothetical tryptophan chain system. There is no experimental evidence that such protocols actually occur in biological microtubules. The work demonstrates mathematical possibility, not biological reality.
```

## 6. McKemmish et al. 2009 Critique

```
Claim: The Orch OR model is not biologically feasible. Two specific claims were made: (I) aromatic molecules cannot switch states because they are delocalized; (II) changes in tubulin protein conformation driven by GTP conversion would require prohibitive energy.
Source: Physical Review E
URL: https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.80.021912
Date: August 2009
Excerpt: "Penrose and Hameroff have argued that the conventional models of a brain function based on neural networks alone cannot account for human consciousness, claiming that quantum-computation elements are also required. Specifically, in their Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch OR) model, it is postulated that microtubules act as quantum processing units, with individual tubulin dimers forming the computational elements. This model requires that the tubulin is able to switch between alternative conformational states in a coherent manner, and that this process be rapid on the physiological time scale. Here, the biological feasibility of the Orch OR proposal is examined in light of recent experimental studies on microtubule assembly and dynamics. It is shown that the tubulins do not possess essential properties required for the Orch OR proposal, as originally proposed, to hold."
Context: First major experimental-physics-based critique of Orch OR, examining tubulin properties against model requirements.
Confidence: HIGH — The paper raises valid physical concerns that were partially addressed by Hameroff & Penrose in subsequent revisions. The critique of GTP-driven switching is widely accepted. The aromatic delocalization point requires more nuanced treatment (see Section 12 on Hameroff-Penrose responses).
```

**Specific Critiques**:
1. **Aromatic delocalization**: McKemmish et al. argued that π electrons in a benzene ring are completely delocalized and cannot switch between states or exist in superposition. This would prevent London force-mediated qubit switching.
2. **GTP hydrolysis energy**: Conformational changes driven by GTP-to-GDP conversion would be prohibitively energy-intensive and generate too much heat for coherent quantum processing.
3. **No quantum computing possible**: "No reformation of the proposal based on known physical paradigms could lead to quantum computing within microtubules."

## 7. Reimers et al. 2009 — Fröhlich Condensation Analysis

```
Claim: Fröhlich condensates are classified into three types: weak (profound effects on chemical kinetics), strong (extremely large energy channeled into one mode), and coherent (energy in a single quantum state). Coherent condensates require extremely large energies, are not produced by the Wu-Austin Hamiltonian, and are extremely fragile — inaccessible in a biological environment. The 8.085 MHz microtubule resonance observed by Pokorny is identified as weak condensation, not the coherence required by Orch OR.
Source: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences
URL: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.0806271106
Date: March 2009
Excerpt: "Coherent condensates are shown to involve extremely large energies, to not be produced by the Wu-Austin dynamical Hamiltonian that provides the simplest depiction of Fröhlich condensates formed using mechanically supplied energy, and to be extremely fragile. They are inaccessible in a biological environment. Hence the Penrose-Hameroff orchestrated objective-reduction model and related theories for cognitive function that embody coherent Fröhlich condensation as an essential element are untenable. Weak condensates, however, may have profound effects on chemical and enzyme kinetics, and may be produced from biochemical energy or from radio frequency, microwave, or terahertz radiation. Pokorný's observed 8.085-MHz microtubulin resonance is identified as a possible candidate."
Context: Comprehensive analysis of Fröhlich condensation regimes and their applicability to biological systems and Orch OR.
Confidence: HIGH — Published in PNAS with rigorous analysis. The distinction between weak, strong, and coherent condensation regimes is physically sound. The conclusion that coherent Fröhlich condensation is inaccessible in biological environments is widely accepted in the physics community. The identification of Pokorny's 8 MHz as weak condensation is important.
```

**Critical Implications**:
- The observed 8.085 MHz resonance (Pokorny 1999, 2001, 2004) could be a weak Fröhlich condensate, but this is "not from any form of coherence required by the Penrose-Hameroff model."
- Reimers et al. calculated that microtubules could only support weak 8 MHz coherence based on linear chain simulations.
- They noted that "the observed decoherence times for quantum processes involving electronic motion are usually in the range of 10 fs to 30 ps" — far shorter than the 25 ms required for Orch OR.

## 8. Tegmark 2000 — The Original Decoherence Calculation

```
Claim: Microtubule entanglement at brain temperatures decoheres on timescales of 10^-13 s (femtoseconds), far too brief for neural processing. The brain is "warm, wet, and noisy" — an environment incompatible with sustained quantum coherence.
Source: Physical Review E (cited in multiple sources)
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9907009 (preprint)
Date: 2000
Excerpt: "Max Tegmark claimed that any quantum coherent system in the brain would undergo effective wave function collapse due to environmental interaction long before it could influence neural processes. He determined the decoherence timescale of microtubule entanglement at brain temperatures to be on the order of femtoseconds, far too brief for neural processing."
Context: The foundational "warm, wet, and noisy" argument that has shaped the debate for 25 years.
Confidence: HIGH — The basic physics of Tegmark's calculation is sound. However, Hameroff et al. (2000) argued that Tegmark mischaracterized the Orch OR model by using incorrect parameters (24 nm superposition separation vs. much smaller separations in Orch OR). This critique has some validity but does not bridge the 12+ orders of magnitude gap to physiologically relevant timescales.
```

## 9. Naskar & Joarder 2023 — Decoherence Timescale Calculations

```
Claim: For a bosonic environment interacting with tubulin dimers in quantum superposition, the decoherence timescale is 1.6 x 10^-21 C0 seconds, where C0 depends on coupling strength and spectral density. For a spin environment, decoherence depends on the ratio Et/d.
Source: arXiv (later published in Quantum Information Processing)
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.06518
Date: April 2023 (arXiv); December 2024 (published)
Excerpt: "For a bosonic environment we obtain that the superposition state decohere at a time scale of 1.60485 x 10^-21 C0 sec. C0 depends on the coupling strength of the interaction and the spectral density. Finding the explicit value of C0 for real environment inside neuron is very important for finding the actual time of decoherence."
Context: Uses standard open quantum system methods (Caldeira-Leggett type) to estimate decoherence for a tubulin superposition state.
Confidence: MEDIUM — The calculation methodology is standard but the exact value of C0 for the neuronal environment remains undetermined. The authors acknowledge that finding C0 is "important as well as difficult." The 10^-21 timescale (with C0 ~ 1) is consistent with Tegmark's order-of-magnitude estimate, but the paper also identifies a regime (Et/d >> 1) where decoherence is suppressed.
```

**Critical Note**: This paper's main contribution is identifying that the spin environment model allows for a parameter regime where decoherence does not dominate. However, the paper does not demonstrate that this regime is actually realized in neurons.

## 10. Kurian et al. 2024 — Tryptophan Superradiance (Experimental)

```
Claim: Mega-networks of tryptophan in protein architectures exhibit collective quantum optical effects (superradiant and subradiant eigenmodes). The fluorescence quantum yield increases with network size, confirming theoretical predictions. The effect persists at thermal equilibrium and in the presence of disorder.
Source: Journal of Physical Chemistry B
URL: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jpcb.3c07936
Date: May 2024
Excerpt: "We analyze the cooperative effects induced by ultraviolet (UV) excitation of several biologically relevant tryptophan mega-networks... Our theoretical analysis in the single-excitation manifold predicts the formation of strongly superradiant states due to collective interactions among organized arrangements of up to more than 10^5 tryptophan UV-excited transition dipoles in microtubule architectures, which leads to an enhancement of the fluorescence quantum yield that is confirmed by our experiments."
Context: Combined computational modeling, theoretical analysis, and experimental measurements of fluorescence quantum yield.
Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH — This is the strongest experimental evidence for quantum effects in microtubule tryptophan networks. The key limitation is that superradiance is a collective radiative phenomenon, not necessarily indicative of quantum information processing. The quantum yield enhancement of a few percent is real but modest. The authors note that "more robust models are needed to account for exciton-phonon couplings in deformations of the protein scaffold."
```

**Key Experimental Findings**:
1. Polymerization from tubulin dimers to microtubules increases quantum yield at 280 nm excitation.
2. Added L-tryptophan reproducibly quenches microtubule fluorescence — a chemically addressable tuning effect.
3. The QY enhancement is "extremely robust to static disorder" — even at 200 cm^-1 disorder strength, QY dependence on MT length is "basically unaffected."
4. However, structural variability "substantially suppresses the brightest cooperative radiative channels relative to idealized ordered lattices."

## 11. Fröhlich Coherence and Microtubules

```
Claim: Fröhlich proposed that metabolic pumping can drive vibrational modes in biological systems into a coherent, collective oscillation — a biological analogue of Bose-Einstein condensation. Microtubules have been proposed as structures particularly suited for Fröhlich coherence due to their dipole-rich cylindrical structure.
Source: Multiple sources (Frohlich 1968; Hameroff et al. 2002; Reimers et al. 2009)
URL: https://doi.org/10.1002/qua.560020825 (Frohlich original)
Date: 1968 (original); subsequent developments through 2025
Excerpt (from Hameroff et al. 2002): "The crystal-like MT structure makes them attractive candidates for quantum coherent excitations, e.g. in the gigaHz range by a mechanism suggested by Fröhlich ('pumped phonons')."
Context: Fröhlich coherence is a prediction, not an established phenomenon. 50+ years of research have not produced unambiguous experimental confirmation.
Confidence: LOW for coherent condensation; MEDIUM for weak condensation — Reimers et al. (2009) established that only weak Fröhlich condensation is accessible biologically. The claim that microtubules exhibit strong Fröhlich coherence at GHz frequencies (Bandyopadhyay) lacks independent replication.
```

## 12. Hameroff-Penrose Responses to Critiques

### 12a. Response to Tegmark (Hameroff et al. 2000)

```
Claim: Tegmark's calculation is not aimed at the actual Orch OR model but at a "hybrid" with incorrect parameters. Correcting Tegmark's equation for superposition separation, charge vs. dipole, and dielectric constant lengthens decoherence time to 10^-5 - 10^-4 s. Metabolic energy ordering water and actin gelation could further extend this to 10^-2 - 10^-1 s.
Source: arXiv quant-ph/0005025
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0005025
Date: May 2000
Excerpt: "recalculation after correcting Tegmark's equation for differences between his model and the 'Orch OR' model (superposition separation, charge vs. dipole, dielectric constant) lengthens the decoherence time to 10^-5 - 10^-4 s and invalidates a critical assumption of Tegmark's derivation."
Context: Direct response to Tegmark 2000, arguing that the Orch OR model has built-in decoherence protection mechanisms.
Confidence: LOW-MEDIUM — The claim of 10^-5 to 10^-4 s decoherence is itself theoretical and depends on assumptions about ordered water and actin gelation that have not been experimentally validated. The further extension to 10^-2 - 10^-1 s is speculative. However, the point that Tegmark's specific calculation used parameters not matching Orch OR is technically valid.
```

### 12b. Response to McKemmish et al. (Hameroff & Penrose 2014)

```
Claim: McKemmish et al. misrepresent the Orch OR tubulin qubit. London forces occur between two or more electron cloud ring structures — a single benzene cannot support London forces. The only tubulin conformational factor in Orch OR is superposition separation at the level of atomic nuclei (femtometer scale), not nanometer-scale conformational changes. No GTP hydrolysis is required.
Source: Physics of Life Reviews
URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1571064513002325
Date: March 2014
Excerpt: "McKemmish et al further assert that tubulin switching in Orch OR requires significant conformational structural change... However, we clarify that tubulin switching in Orch OR need not actually involve significant conformational change, that electron cloud dipole states (London forces) are sufficient for bit-like switching, superposition and qubit function... The dominant effect is 2) separation at the level of atomic nuclei, e.g. 2.5 Fermi length for carbon nuclei (2.5 femtometers; 2.5 x 10^-15 meters)."
Context: Major revision of Orch OR to avoid the McKemmish et al. critiques by shifting from conformational to electronic-scale superposition.
Confidence: MEDIUM — The response is technically coherent but raises new questions. Femtometer-scale nuclear separation as a qubit basis is physically unusual and its connection to consciousness remains entirely speculative. The claim that "it takes two (or more) to tango" regarding London forces between aromatic rings is physically correct, but whether such dipole arrangements can support the required coherence times remains unverified.
```

Claim: Bandyopadhyay's gigahertz, megahertz, and kilohertz resonance in microtubules is "clear evidence for Fröhlich condensation, e.g., mediated by oscillating London force dipoles or spin currents." The conformational-switch qubit was never actually part of Orch OR except in "cartoon illustrations."

Large scale (nanometer) conformational flexing never was part of Orch OR except in cartoon illustrations."
Context: Defense against Reimers et al.'s claim that Fröhlich condensation cannot support Orch OR.
Confidence: LOW — The claim that Bandyopadhyay's resonance data represents Fröhlich condensation is disputed. Reimers et al. identified the 8 MHz resonance as weak condensation only. Bandyopadhyay's GHz claims have not been independently replicated. The assertion that nanometer conformational flexing "never was part of Orch OR" contradicts early Orch OR publications and illustrations.

## 15. Overall Assessment

### 15.1 What is Established

1. **Tryptophan networks in microtubules exhibit collective optical behavior**. The Kurian et al. 2024 experimental confirmation of fluorescence quantum yield enhancement in tryptophan mega-networks is the strongest evidence for quantum effects in microtubules. This is a real quantum optical phenomenon.

2. **Superradiant and subradiant states exist in idealized MT models**. Multiple theoretical groups (Celardo 2019, Kurian 2024, PMC 2025) have confirmed this using standard quantum optical formalism.

3. **Microtubules have interesting electromagnetic properties**. The 8.085 MHz resonance (Pokorny) is experimentally observed and consistent with weak Fröhlich condensation.

### 15.2 What is Controversial

1. **The QED cavity model** with 10^-6 s decoherence depends critically on assumptions about ordered water that are not experimentally validated.

2. **Bandyopadhyay's GHz resonance claims** have not been independently replicated and are not accepted by the broader physics community.

3. **The functional significance of subradiant states** for biological information processing remains entirely speculative.

### 15.3 What is Critically Challenged

1. **Orch OR as a theory of consciousness** faces fundamental objections from decoherence physics. Even Hameroff & Penrose's revised decoherence estimates (10^-5 - 10^-4 s) fall far short of the 25 ms required for consciousness-relevant timescales.

2. **Quantum computation in microtubules** has no experimental evidence. The Mavromatos 2025 proposal for "scalable ambient-temperature quantum computation" is entirely theoretical.

3. **Quantum teleportation in tryptophan chains** (Shirmovsky) is a mathematical formalism without biological validation.

### 15.4 Key Open Questions

1. What is the actual extent of water ordering inside the microtubule lumen?
2. Can the 10^-6 s decoherence time be experimentally tested via Rabi splitting spectroscopy?
3. What is the functional role (if any) of superradiant UV fluorescence in cellular signaling?
4. Can Bandyopadhyay's multi-frequency resonance data be independently replicated?
5. Do subradiant states play any role in neuronal information processing?
