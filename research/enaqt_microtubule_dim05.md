### 1.2 Cryo-EM Microtubule Structures — Nucleotide States

Claim: High-resolution cryo-EM structures of microtubules in three distinct states were solved: GMPCPP (4.7 Å, mimicking GTP state), GDP (4.9 Å), and taxol-stabilized (5.6 Å). The structures reveal that GTP hydrolysis results in compaction of the E-site at the inter-dimer interface coupled to conformational changes in α-tubulin.
Source: Cell (Nogales lab) / PMC
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4054694/
Date: 2014
Excerpt: "Cryo-EM maps of GMPCPP (left panel, 4.7 Å resolution), GDP (middle panel, 4.9 Å resolution) and taxol-stabilized (right panel, 5.6 Å resolution) microtubules, viewed from inside the microtubule lumen... Hydrolysis results in a compression of the E-site at the inter-dimer interface... The structural change is dominated by a translation of 1.28 Å towards the plus end, moving as a unit with the C-terminal base of H7, effectively tucking up into the dimer."
Context: These structures (PDB entries including 3JAT for GMPCPP and 3JAS for GDP) represent the first near-atomic resolution cryo-EM structures of microtubules in different nucleotide states. The comparison revealed how GTP hydrolysis generates internal strain within the tubulin dimer that is released during depolymerization.
Confidence: High

---

Claim: Cryo-EM structures of dynamic and stabilized microtubules revealed that straight tubulin conformation is found within the microtubule body, while a curved conformation is found in depolymerization peels. PDB entries 3JAT (GMPCPP) and 3JAS (GDP) represent the straight conformation states.
Source: High-Resolution Microtubule Structures (Cell 2014)
URL: https://www.lander-lab.com/pdfs/24855948.pdf
Date: 2014
Excerpt: "A straight tubulin conformation is found within the body of the microtubule (Li et al., 2002; Nogales et al., 1999)... A curved conformation is found in microtubule depolymerization peels (Mandelkow et al., 1991), head-to-tail arrays of bent tubulin heterodimers wherein longitudinal contacts are maintained after lateral contacts are broken."
Context: Landmark study from the Nogales lab that provided the first high-resolution comparison of microtubules in GTP-like (GMPCPP) vs GDP states. The GMPCPP/GDP models show an overall RMSD of 0.75 Å, with larger changes in α-tubulin (0.86 Å) than β-tubulin (0.63 Å).
Confidence: High

Source: Multiple sources / bioRxiv preprint

Context: PDB 1SA0 (Ravelli et al., 2004) contains the structure of tubulin bound to a stathmin-like domain, showing the curved conformation that tubulin adopts upon depolymerization. This is the reference structure for the "bent" tubulin state.

## 2. AlphaFold Models of Tubulin and MAPs

Claim: AlphaFold Protein Structure Database contains predicted structures for all major tubulin isoforms. Tubulin alpha and beta chains are predicted with very high confidence (pLDDT > 90 for most residues) due to their well-defined globular folds. However, the C-terminal tails (residues ~437-451 in α-tubulin, ~426-445 in β-tubulin) show low pLDDT scores (<50) consistent with their intrinsically disordered nature.
Source: AlphaFold DB / Frontiers in Cellular Neuroscience
URL: https://alphafold.ebi.ac.uk/entry/B2RSN3
Date: 2022
Excerpt: "Last updated in AlphaFold DB version 2022-11-01, created with the AlphaFold Monomer v2.0 pipeline... Context: AlphaFold provides predictions for all human tubulin isotypes including TUBA1A (P68363), TUBA1B (Q71U36), TUBA1C (Q9BSJ2), TUBA4A (P68366), TUBB (P07437), TUBB2A (Q13885), TUBB2B (Q9BVA1), TUBB3 (Q13509), TUBB4A (P04350), TUBB4B (P68371), and TUBG1 (Q9NRH3). The Predicted Aligned Error (PAE) plots are useful for assessing confidence in relative domain positions.

## 3. Cryo-EM Structures of Microtubules in Different States

### 3.1 Nucleotide States

Claim: The Nogales lab solved the first high-resolution cryo-EM structures of microtubules in three distinct nucleotide states: GMPCPP (GTP analog, 4.7 Å), GDP (4.9 Å), and taxol-stabilized GDP (5.6 Å). The structures reveal subtle but functionally important conformational changes at the inter-dimer interface upon GTP hydrolysis.
Source: Cell / Lander lab PDF
URL: https://www.lander-lab.com/pdfs/24855948.pdf
Date: 2014-2015
Excerpt: "The overall rmsd between the GMPCPP and GDP models, computed from optimally aligned dimers, is 0.75 Å. Larger rmsd changes are observed within the α subunit (0.86 Å) than within the β subunit (0.63 Å). Interestingly, larger structural changes occur in the alpha subunit C-terminal half compared to the N-terminal half."
Context: These structures demonstrate that GTP hydrolysis causes compaction at the E-site (inter-dimer interface) and internal strain in α-tubulin that would be released during depolymerization. The key PDB entries are 3JAT (GMPCPP) and 3JAS (GDP).
Confidence: High

### 3.2 MD Simulation Tubulin Structures — Straight vs. Kinked

Claim: Six tubulin structures were selected for molecular dynamics simulations comparing straight dimers (PDB: 3JAT for GMPCPP, 3JAS for GDP) and kinked dimers (PDB: 5JQG, 5FNV for GTP states; 4ZHQ, 4ZOL for GDP states). These structures capture both the straight protofilament conformation in the microtubule lattice and the kinked conformations at the microtubule ends.
Source: eLife (tubulin allostery study)
URL: https://elifesciences.org/articles/34353v1.pdf
Date: 2018
Excerpt: "Six tubulin structures were selected for subsequent MD simulations: two straight dimers (PDB IDs: 3JAT (GMPCPP), 3JAS (GDP)) and four kinked dimers (PDB IDs: 5JQG (GTP), 5FNV (GTP), 4ZHQ (GDP), 4ZOL (GDP)). For convenience, we refer to the straight structures as TUB^GT^ and TUB^GP^, and to the kinked structures as TUB^GT^ and TUB^GP^."

## 4. Microscopy Data — TIRF and Dynamics Measurements

### 4.1 TIRF Microtubule Dynamics Assays

Claim: TIRF microscopy is the standard method for measuring microtubule dynamics in vitro. Typical assays use GMPCPP-stabilized seeds, fluorescently labeled tubulin (5-20% labeled), and imaging at 37°C to extract parameters including growth/shrinkage rates, catastrophe frequency, and rescue frequency.
Source: Measuring Microtubule Dynamics (PMC)
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6281472/
Date: 2018
Excerpt: "Typically, dynamic microtubules are assembled from a template... short stable microtubule seeds are polymerized using the non-hydrolyzable GTP analogue GMPCPP. Seeds are attached to the coverslip either using antibodies against a fluorophore or via streptavidin linking to biotin-tubulin... The specific immobilization of the template and passivation of the remaining coverslip surface result in the dynamic microtubule being free of, but in close proximity to, the surface thereby allowing TIRF microscopy."
Context: This is the standard method for in vitro microtubule dynamics measurement. Growth speeds typically range from 0.4-3 μm/min depending on tubulin concentration (6-20 μM). Parameters extracted include: average growth/shrinkage velocity, catastrophe frequency, rescue frequency, and time spent in growth/shrinkage/pause phases.
Confidence: High

---

Source: Scientific Reports (Nature)
URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-018-37767-1
Date: 2019-03-07
Excerpt: "Movies analysed by MTrack have been taken from polymerization reactions with Xenopus laevis, Xenopus tropicalis and Bos taurus tubulin ranging from 6-20 μM total tubulin with growth speeds ranging from 0.4 μm/min to 3 μm/min... Images were taken on the motorized inverted Nikon Eclipse Ti-E microscope with a motorized TIRF angle... equipped with a Nikon Plan Apochromat 100x/1.5NA oil immersion objective lens and an EMCCD, Andor iXon Ultra X3 987 Camera."
Context: The MTrack software and associated datasets are publicly available. The experimental setup described represents a standard configuration for TIRF-based microtubule dynamics assays.
Confidence: High

### 4.2 Typical Microtubule Dynamics Parameters

Claim: Published datasets of microtubule dynamics parameters report typical values: plus-end growth rate 0.2-4 μm/min, catastrophe frequency 0.001-0.002 s⁻¹ (0.06-0.12 min⁻¹) at high tubulin concentrations, and a GTP cap length of approximately 40-80 nm (~70 tubulin dimers).
Source: Physical Review E / bioRxiv
URL: http://rpdata.caltech.edu/courses/aph161/Handouts/Flyvbjerg1996.pdf
Date: 1996 (model); 2020 (updated measurements)
Excerpt: "The catastrophe rates reported are... f_cat^(+)^(c→∞) = 0.001-0.002 s⁻¹, or 0.06-0.12 min⁻¹... the length of the 'GTP cap' is about 70 nm, at least for MT polymerized in vitro. This is consistent with a recent study by McIntosh et al. which determined the length of the curved protofilament ends to be about 40-80 nm."
Context: These values come from fitting theoretical models to experimental data. The catastrophe rate is concentration-dependent, increasing at lower tubulin concentrations. The GTP cap size has been indirectly measured through EB1 comet intensity correlation.
Confidence: High

## 5. Molecular Motors on Microtubules

### 5.1 Kinesin-1

Claim: The cryo-EM structure of kinesin-1 bound to microtubules in the ADP state (PDB: 8RIK) was solved at 3.3 Å, revealing a Mg-free apo-like ADP pre-release conformation. This structure represents a missing state in the two-step allosteric mechanism of ADP release.
Source: Nature Communications
URL: https://www.rcsb.org/structure/8RIK
Date: 2024-11-20 (published 2025)
Excerpt: "Microtubule association induces a Mg-free apo-like ADP pre-release conformation in kinesin-1 that is unaffected by its autoinhibitory tail... MT association remodels the hydrogen bond network at the nucleotide binding site triggering removal of the Mg²⁺ ion from the Mg²⁺-ADP complex."
Context: Kinesin-1 (PDB: 8RIK, 2024) represents the most complete atomic model of the kinesin-1 motor domain on microtubules. Previous key structures include 1IA0, 2HXF, and 3ZFD for various kinesin-microtubule complexes.
Confidence: High

## 6. Plus-End Tracking Proteins (+TIPs)

Claim: EB1 structure comprises a calponin-homology (CH) domain for microtubule binding, a coiled-coil region for homodimerization, and an EB homology (EBH) domain for binding other +TIPs via the SxIP motif. EB1 forms autonomous comets at growing microtubule plus ends.
Source: Journal of Cell Science / PMC
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5418063/
Date: 2017
Excerpt: "The structure of EB1 comprises a calponin-homology domain responsible for microtubule binding, a coiled-coil region, which mediates its homodimerization, and a unique EB homology (EBH) domain for binding to other +TIPs... In the SxIP-EB1 complexes, the Ile/Leu-Pro dipeptide of the SxIP motif is inserted into the hydrophobic pocket formed by the EBH domain."
Context: EB1 cryo-EM structures on microtubule plus ends have been solved at ~4.5 Å resolution (PDB: 5MFP, 5MHD). The CH domain binds to the microtubule lattice in a groove between protofilaments. EB1 recognizes a specific structural state of tubulin at growing microtubule ends.
Confidence: High
