```
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8282191/
Date: 2021
Excerpt: "2DES is a nonlinear laser spectroscopy method with ultrafast time and frequency resolution, and it provided more accurate descriptions of coherence, coupling, and dynamics in a system than traditional linear spectroscopy... Context: Review article discussing 20 years of 2DES development and its transition from quantum biology to quantum technology applications.
Confidence: high
```

```

URL: https://arxiv.org/html/2511.14363v1

Excerpt: "2DES is an ultrafast laser spectroscopy technique that offers greater resolution compared to traditional linear spectroscopy, enabling the elucidation of coherence, coupling, and dynamic processes within a system... Context: Comprehensive review of quantum coherence in photosynthesis and related quantum simulation approaches.
Confidence: high
```

## 15. Critical Assessment and Gaps <a name="15-assessment"></a>

### Established Techniques Successfully Applied to Biological Quantum Systems

1. **2DES**: Definitively demonstrated quantum coherence in photosynthetic complexes at 77 K and physiological temperature. **Not yet applied to microtubules.** Technical challenges: UV excitation required for tryptophan (~280 nm), which is more difficult than visible-range photosynthetic pigments. fs UV pulses can cause sample damage.

2. **FLIM/Stern-Volmer quenching**: Successfully demonstrated energy migration in microtubules (Kalra 2023). Readily available technique. Does not directly measure quantum coherence but provides evidence for exciton transport.

3. **Superradiance QY**: Initial experimental evidence from Kurian group (2024). Needs complementary lifetime measurements to confirm radiative rate enhancement.

4. **AC Impedance**: Well-established technique showing unexpected resonant features at ~39 Hz. Reproducible across groups but interpretation in terms of quantum effects remains speculative.

### Proposed but Not Yet Demonstrated for Microtubules

1. **Rabi-splitting spectroscopy**: Detailed theoretical proposal exists (Nair et al. 2025) with concrete parameter estimates. Requires THz cavity setup with microtubule samples. Major technical challenge: achieving strong coupling in aqueous environment.

2. **Entangled photon/surface plasmon probes**: Proposed for detecting quantum coherence in individual tubulin dimers. Requires sophisticated quantum optics setup integrated with biological sample handling.

3. **Delayed luminescence**: Proposed by Tuszynski/Hameroff to determine decoherence times. May be confounded by phosphorescence and other long-lived emission processes in proteins.

4. **NV center sensing**: Proven for neuronal action potentials but not yet applied to detect microtubule-specific signals. Challenge: targeting nanodiamonds to microtubules and distinguishing MT signals from background.

5. **SQUID magnetometry**: Predicted by Orch OR (1998) but never demonstrated for microtubules. Extremely challenging due to weak magnetic signals from biomolecules.

### Key Experimental Gaps

- No direct measurement of electronic quantum coherence (oscillatory signals) in microtubule tryptophan networks has been reported
- Temperature dependence of energy migration in MTs has not been studied
- No 2DES study of microtubule tryptophan networks exists
- The role of ordered water in microtubule quantum effects remains controversial and experimentally unresolved
- Single-molecule studies of tubulin conformational quantum superpositions have not been performed
- The relationship between anesthetic binding sites and tryptophan network topology needs structural characterization
